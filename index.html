steamdeck good for homebrew

i followed a steam deck optimization guide this weekend. i played with different setups and generally scrutinized the product.

firstly, it nails what it's primarily trying to do:
be a handheld pc game system

it allows for docking with a tv, but it's not powerful enough to replace your xbox or your playstation. it's not going to replace your desktop or laptop either. i probably won't use it docked any further.

its ability to switch back and forth between desktop mode (kde plasma) and gaming mode works nicely. desktop mode acts as a power-user overflow valve: it's where all of your customizations and tweaks happen.

distributing for the steam deck locally was extremely simple:

  1. scp myapp steamdeck.local:~/mybrew
  2. ssh steamdeck.local 'steam-os-add-to-steam ~/mybrew/myapp'

after that, it's available from the "non-steam games" category. you don't even have to drop into desktop mode.

the steam deck ran a bevy game and a tauri v2 app without any tweaks. to ship the tauri app, use the AppImage created by the tauri build command.

back to its gaming features ...

the games that are going to work the best for it are those built with a gamepad in mind.

that being said, if you're willing to mess around with controller settings for a game (which is easy to do via steam input), a lot seems to be possible.

steam deck otw

ngl i am a little excited over the idea of hobby indie games for the steam deck. it's an expensive homebrew system, but considering that homebrew systems haven't really taken off ... maybe this is the direction needed.

i wouldn't be surprised if bevy developers lined up for the steam deck if there were a solid path for publishing their projects on it.

xbox, playstation, and nintendo have yet to pick up the ball on indie rust games.

homebrew game development needs a console equivalent to the road to indy.
